# Appearance and palettes

Open **Settings** and scroll past the location section. Two appearance controls follow: a dark/light toggle and a palette grid.

## Dark vs light

Two large mode cards.

- **Dark** — the default. Low-light surfaces with deep, near-black backgrounds. Designed to be readable while you're also at the eyepiece or under a red headlamp.
- **Light** — high-contrast pale surfaces. Useful during daytime planning, or on a glossy display where dark mode reflects too much.

The whole UI flips instantly. The choice persists for the rest of the session.

## Palettes

Seven palettes ship with the app, each with its own dark and light variant:

| Palette    | Mood                                                    |
|------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| Aurora     | Cool teal-green default                                 |
| Nebula     | Warm pink-violet                                        |
| Mars       | Rust and copper                                         |
| Ember      | Deep amber                                              |
| Verdant    | Forest green                                            |
| Monochrome | Pure grayscale — no accent hue                          |
| Solar      | Bright golden yellow                                    |

The palette grid in *Settings → Color palette* shows a small preview card for each — surface color, a sample card, two accent dots, and a gradient bar. The checked card is the active palette.

Clicking a card switches the palette immediately. The change is animated across the whole UI: surfaces, accent glows, table heatmap colors, and the boot splash logomark all update.

> The visibility heatmap in the planner uses the active accent color for the high-score end of the gradient. Switching palettes is a quick way to find one whose heatmap is most legible to you.

## What the toggle on the top bar does

The sun/moon icon in the top-right corner of the app is the same control as *Settings → Appearance*. It only flips dark/light — the palette stays where you left it.
